HTTPS

The American people expect government websites to be secure and their interactions with those websites to be private. If websites use the unencrypted HTTP protocol, they are not providing a secure and private connection to visitors.

All publicly accessible federal websites are required to transition to using the encrypted HTTPS protocol, which protects website data from interception and alteration while it is sent to and from visitors.

Analytics

All federal agencies need to understand how people use their websites, and adding analytics is an easy way to get useful and surprising data to make better decisions. People use many agency websites, so to share insights into visitor needs, all agencies should use the Digital Analytics Program.

This is a no-cost service that all agencies can opt into to collect anonymized visitor data, such as how many visitors use mobile devices. Participating agencies can also view detailed visitor data from fellow participants.
